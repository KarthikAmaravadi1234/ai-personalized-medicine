# High Blood Pressure (Hypertension)

Source: Adapted from CDC and NIH public health education materials (educational summary).

Blood pressure is the force of blood pushing against the walls of the arteries. High
blood pressure, or hypertension, means this force is consistently too high, which makes
the heart work harder and damages arteries over time. It is a major risk factor for
heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease.

## What the numbers mean

Blood pressure is written as systolic over diastolic in millimeters of mercury (mmHg).
Categories for adults are:

- Normal: less than 120 and less than 80
- Elevated: 120 to 129 systolic and less than 80 diastolic
- Stage 1 hypertension: 130 to 139 systolic or 80 to 89 diastolic
- Stage 2 hypertension: 140 or higher systolic or 90 or higher diastolic

## Managing blood pressure

Reducing sodium intake, staying physically active, limiting alcohol, maintaining a
healthy weight, and not smoking all help control blood pressure. When lifestyle changes
are insufficient, medicines such as ACE inhibitors, diuretics, or calcium channel
blockers may be prescribed.
