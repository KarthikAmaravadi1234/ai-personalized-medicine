# Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c)

Source: Adapted from CDC and NIH public health education materials (educational summary).

The hemoglobin A1c test, often written HbA1c or simply A1c, reflects average blood
glucose over the previous two to three months. Because it captures a longer window
than a single glucose reading, it is widely used to diagnose and monitor diabetes.

## What the numbers mean

HbA1c is reported as a percentage. Commonly used thresholds for adults are:

- Normal: below 5.7 percent
- Prediabetes: 5.7 to 6.4 percent
- Diabetes: 6.5 percent or higher on two separate tests

For many adults with diabetes, a treatment target of below 7 percent is common, though
individual goals vary with age, other conditions, and risk of low blood sugar.

## Improving HbA1c

Lowering HbA1c usually involves a combination of dietary changes, physical activity,
weight management, and, when needed, glucose-lowering medication such as metformin or
insulin. Small sustained reductions in HbA1c can meaningfully lower the risk of
long-term complications.
