# Type 2 Diabetes

Source: Adapted from CDC and NIH public health education materials (educational summary).

Type 2 diabetes is a chronic condition in which the body does not use insulin well and
cannot keep blood glucose at normal levels. It develops over many years and is strongly
linked to excess body weight, physical inactivity, and genetic risk factors.

## Risk factors

Common risk factors include being overweight or obese, being age 45 or older, having a
parent or sibling with type 2 diabetes, being physically active fewer than three times a
week, and having had gestational diabetes. Some ethnic groups have higher risk.

## Symptoms and diagnosis

Early type 2 diabetes often has no symptoms. When present, symptoms can include increased
thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, and blurred vision. Diagnosis uses blood tests such
as fasting glucose, HbA1c, or an oral glucose tolerance test.

## Prevention and management

Modest weight loss, a balanced diet, and at least 150 minutes of moderate activity per
week can prevent or delay type 2 diabetes in people at high risk. For those already
diagnosed, management combines lifestyle changes with medications like metformin and
regular monitoring of glucose and HbA1c.
