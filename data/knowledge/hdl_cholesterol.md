# HDL Cholesterol

Source: Adapted from CDC and NIH public health education materials (educational summary).

HDL (high-density lipoprotein) cholesterol is often called "good" cholesterol. HDL
helps carry cholesterol away from the arteries and back to the liver, where it is
removed from the body. Higher HDL levels are generally associated with lower
cardiovascular risk.

## What the numbers mean

HDL cholesterol is measured in milligrams per deciliter (mg/dL). General guidance for
adults is:

- Low (higher risk): less than 40 mg/dL for men and less than 50 mg/dL for women
- Acceptable: 40 to 59 mg/dL
- Protective: 60 mg/dL and above

## Raising HDL

Regular aerobic exercise, avoiding tobacco smoke, and replacing trans fats with
healthy unsaturated fats can modestly raise HDL. Unlike LDL, there is no medication
whose primary purpose is simply to raise HDL; the focus is on overall cardiovascular
risk reduction.
